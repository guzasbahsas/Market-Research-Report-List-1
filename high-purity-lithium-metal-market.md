<p><p>The high-purity lithium metal market primarily consists of two types: Salt Lake Brine and Lithium Ore. Salt Lake Brine is extracted from large saline lakes, where lithium is concentrated through evaporation processes, resulting in high-purity yields. In contrast, Lithium Ore, obtained from hard rock mining, involves the extraction of lithium-bearing minerals such as spodumene. Both sources play vital roles in meeting the growing demand for lithium in batteries and other advanced technologies, driving market dynamics and pricing.</p></p>
