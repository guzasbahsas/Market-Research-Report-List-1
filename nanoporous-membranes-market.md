<p><p>The nanoporous membranes market is divided into two primary types: organic and inorganic nanoporous membranes. Organic nanoporous membranes are typically crafted from polymer materials, offering flexibility and ease of fabrication, making them ideal for applications like filtration and separation processes. In contrast, inorganic nanoporous membranes, made from materials such as ceramics or metals, provide enhanced thermal and chemical stability, making them suitable for more demanding environments. Both types play crucial roles in various industries, including water purification, gas separation, and energy storage.</p></p>
