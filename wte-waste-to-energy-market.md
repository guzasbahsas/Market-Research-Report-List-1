<p><p>Waste-to-Energy (WTE) conversion involves generating energy from waste materials, primarily through two main market types: incineration and other technologies. Incineration involves burning waste at high temperatures to produce steam for electricity generation, effectively reducing waste volume and minimizing landfill use. Other WTE technologies include anaerobic digestion, gasification, and pyrolysis, which break down organic waste or convert it into usable gas or oil. Each method provides an efficient way to manage waste while generating renewable energy, supporting sustainable practices.</p></p>
