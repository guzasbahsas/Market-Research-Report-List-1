<p><p>The Overactive Bladder Treatment Market encompasses therapies for both idiopathic and neurogenic overactive bladder conditions. Idiopathic overactive bladder, characterized by urgency and frequency without an identifiable cause, primarily benefits from anticholinergic medications and lifestyle modifications. Conversely, neurogenic overactive bladder, resulting from neurological conditions like multiple sclerosis or spinal cord injury, may require a combination of pharmacological treatments, neuromodulation therapies, and catheterization. The market focuses on innovative drug development and advanced therapeutic devices to improve patient outcomes across these distinct applications.</p></p>
